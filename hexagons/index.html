A party trick:

The Hex Map

During the brief-ish sabbatical that I started in 2023 (and which will hopefully end during early 2024), one night I was at a party where I knew very few people. Grabbing some ice, I started chatting with a guy I'd had a small interaction with before.

"So, what do you do for a living?"

"I'm a game developer!"

About twenty minutes later, me and this guy were holding board markers and drawing hexagons on the wall while more and more curious people gathered around asking what on Earth we were talking about. We were talking about hexagons!

The concept was simple:
  • There is a map made up of hexagons
  • Each hexagon has an intrinsic quality (let's call it its 'element')
  • Each side that's shared by two hexagons has a property (let's call it its 'powerup') that's the product of the qualities of the two hexagons that are touching

"I'm thinking of building each hexagon as an object with six individual sides, and then figure out how to assign hexagons to sides. I don't know."

"Wait, no, I think... have you tried representing it as an array? This should follow a few simple rules and save you a lot of trouble"

In the end, we came up with a simple algorithm that both
  • Represents the hexagon map in a simple data structure
  • Is able to easily determine which other hexagons a hexagon is touching
